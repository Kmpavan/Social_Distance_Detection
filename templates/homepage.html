SOCIAL DISTANCING STOPS THE SPREAD OF DISEASE
  • 01 What it means to practice "Social distancing"

    COVID-19 is mainly spread through person-to-person contact. This means in close quarters, a non-infected person can inhale respiratory droplets from an infected person, especially whenever they cough or sneeze. According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), social distancing means remaining out of group settings and avoiding mass gatherings. When near other people, maintaining a distance of at least six feet from them when possible is critical as well

  • 02 The "importance" of social distancing

    The goal of social distancing is to keep healthy people and those who are sick from coming into close contact with one another. This slows the person-to-person transmission of the virus, as well as the spread of COVID-19 in general. When everyone is not sick all at once, hospitals aren’t overwhelmed and can better manage the resources needed to care for infected individuals

  • 03 "Social distancing " tips

    Practicing social distancing means you’re doing your part to prevent the spread of COVID-19. However, staying at home can start to get lonely after a while

    Check out these tips to keep busy and stay connected with others.

    1.Check things off your to-do list: -This could include cleaning around the house, writing thank-you cards, organizing personal paperwork or getting to those around-the-house projects.
    2.Stay active while at home: -There are so many ways to work out at home thanks to the internet.
    3.Keep a routine: - Keep a routine at home that is similar to your normal schedule.
    4.Make time for self-improvement and care: - Read up on something you’re interested in and Learn something new, like a language or a skill.
    5.Keep in touch with other people who may be feeling disconnected too: -Call a grandparent or relative you haven’t talked to in a while. Use video calls to feel more present when reaching out to friends or your family.
